Veritas ZK — Zero-Knowledge Credential Verifier

Prove you're compliant — "I'm 18+", "my country is allowed", "this credential is valid & mine" — without revealing the underlying document. A Groth16 proof of a derived predicate, verified on-chain by a Soroban contract via a real BN254 pairing.

Circom Groth16 / BN254 Soroban Stellar
The ZK Proof Flow
Step 1

Issuer Signs

A trusted attester (KYC, government…) signs a credential claim with a BabyJub EdDSA-Poseidon signature and publishes its public key on-chain.

→
Step 2

Holder Proves

The user runs the off-chain prover (circom + snarkjs) and emits a Groth16 proof of a derived predicate — DOB, country & identity stay private.

→
Step 3

Chain Verifies

The Soroban contract runs the BN254 pairing check in WASM, binds issuer + Merkle root, records a nullifier, and unlocks the compliant action.

Why This Wins

The angle

Compliance / KYC is the #1 real-world barrier to on-chain payments. Today, proving you're allowed to transact means uploading a passport to a centralized oracle. Veritas replaces that with cryptographic proof of a derived predicate: the verifier learns only that the predicate holds — never the underlying attribute.

What makes it special

  • One design lands three use-cases: identity verification, private payments, confidential tokens.
  • The clearest demo-able ZK story — a binary allow / deny outcome.
  • Real BN254 pairing executed on-chain in ~73 KB of WASM (no native precompile on Stellar).
  • The same pairing core is unit-tested against real snarkjs proofs.

CredentialAge *issuer-signed credential whose private age ≥ threshold — combines all of the above

Primitives: Poseidon hash · EdDSA over BabyJubJub · Groth16 on BN254 — all from iden3/circomlib. An invalid predicate makes the circuit unsatisfiable: no proof can ever exist.
