## Description

Veritas ZK is a privacy-preserving credential-compliance layer for Stellar / Soroban. A holder generates a Groth16 zero-knowledge proof that a derived predicate about their credential holds — "I'm 18+", "this credential is mine and valid", "my country is in the allow-set" — and a Soroban smart contract verifies that proof on-chain via a real BN254 pairing. The verifier learns only that the predicate holds; the date of birth, country, name, and underlying document never leave the holder's device.

Why this matters: KYC, AML, and sanctions compliance is the single biggest barrier to real-world on-chain payments. Today, proving you may transact means uploading a passport to a centralized oracle — a privacy and custody nightmare. ZK credentials replace document disclosure with cryptographic proof: the contract learns "this person is 18+", never their date of birth. An invalid predicate makes the circuit unsatisfiable — no proof can ever exist — so forgery is impossible, not merely discouraged.

How it works: an issuer signs a credential claim with EdDSA-Poseidon over BabyJubJub. The holder derives a predicate and produces a Groth16 proof in circom on top of circomlib (Poseidon hash, BabyJubJub, Groth16 over BN254). Because Stellar has no BN254 precompile, Veritas implements the full Groth16 verification — miller-loop and final-exponentiation — inside a `#![no_std]` Soroban contract using `ark-bn254`, exposing `verify_for(circuit_id, proof, public)` and `verify_credential` (proof + issuer-pubkey match + Merkle-root match + nullifier record for double-spend protection).

One signed credential yields reusable proofs across dApps. A stablecoin issuer can gate transfers by jurisdiction; an exchange can prove accreditation without PII; a DeFi protocol can enforce sanctions screening via an on-chain allow-set root. One design lands identity verification, private payments, and confidential tokens at once — with every byte the contract sees generated by the open pipeline, no stubs and no fake "verified" flags.

## How It Works

1. **Issue** — An issuer signs a credential claim with EdDSA-Poseidon over BabyJubJub (BabyJubJub lives inside the BN254 scalar field, so signatures compose cleanly with Groth16 proofs).
2. **Prove** — The holder derives a predicate ("I'm 18+", "my country is in the allow-set") and generates a Groth16 proof in circom atop circomlib — Poseidon hash, BabyJubJub EdDSA, Groth16 over BN254. The credential document, DOB, and name never leave the holder's device.
3. **Verify on-chain** — Because Stellar has no BN254 precompile, Veritas implements full Groth16 verification (miller-loop + final-exponentiation) inside a `#![no_std]` Soroban contract using `ark-bn254`. It exposes `verify_for(circuit_id, proof, public)` and `verify_credential` (proof validity + issuer-pubkey match + Merkle-root match + nullifier record for double-spend protection).
4. **Gate** — A verified proof unlocks gated actions (faucet, transfer, token mint) with no PII ever disclosed. An unsatisfiable predicate produces no proof — forgery is impossible, not merely discouraged.

## Circuits

Three circom circuits built on circomlib:

1. **Age verification** — prove age ≥ threshold from a private date-of-birth, without revealing the DOB.
2. **Credential issuance** — prove a credential was issuer-signed and is owned by the prover, emitting a double-spend nullifier.
3. **Range proof** — prove a private value lies within a range (e.g. jurisdiction allow-set membership via Poseidon Merkle proof).

All over BN254: Poseidon hash, BabyJubJub EdDSA, Groth16.
